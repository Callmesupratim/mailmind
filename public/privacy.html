Privacy Policy

Mailmind  ·  Last updated: June 15, 2026

Overview

Mailmind is a desktop email client that connects to your email accounts (Gmail, Microsoft Outlook, Zoho, Yahoo, or any IMAP mailbox) and optionally uses AI services to analyse email threads and draft replies. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how it is protected.

Mailmind runs entirely on your computer. Your email data is never sent to Mailmind servers — there are none. All data stays on your machine or travels directly between your device and the email and AI providers you choose.

Data the app accesses

When you connect an email account, Mailmind accesses:

  • Your email address (used to label the account)
  • Email message headers, subjects, senders, dates, and body content
  • Attachment metadata and content (when you open a message)
  • Folder and label structure
  • Read/unread status and starred/flagged status

Mailmind uses Gmail API scopes gmail.modify and gmail.compose to read your messages, archive/trash/star threads, mark messages as read, create drafts, and send replies — all in direct response to actions you take in the app.

How your data is used

Email content is used solely to:

  • Display your messages in the Mailmind interface
  • Execute actions you request (archive, reply, star, trash, send)
  • Generate AI analysis or draft replies when you explicitly trigger an AI feature

Your email data is never used for advertising, profiling, training AI models, or shared with any third party except the AI provider you select when you trigger an AI feature.

Data storage

Mailmind stores the following data locally on your computer:

  • OAuth access tokens and refresh tokens for connected accounts — encrypted with AES-256-GCM before being written to a local SQLite database
  • IMAP/SMTP credentials (for non-OAuth accounts) — also encrypted at rest
  • AI analysis results — cached locally to avoid repeated API calls for the same thread
  • Your AI provider preferences and API keys — encrypted at rest

No email messages or attachments are stored locally beyond what is held in memory while the app is open.

AI features and third-party providers

AI analysis and reply drafting are optional. When you use them, the content of the email thread you are viewing is sent to the AI provider you have selected in Settings. Supported providers:

  • Groq (default) — groq.com/privacy-policy
  • OpenAI — openai.com/policies/privacy-policy
  • Anthropic — anthropic.com/privacy
  • Google Gemini — policies.google.com/privacy
  • Mistral — mistral.ai/privacy-policy

You supply your own API keys for these providers. Mailmind does not proxy or record API calls.

Gmail API disclosure: Mailmind's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Data obtained via the Gmail API is used only within the app to display and manage your emails and is not transferred to any party other than the AI provider you explicitly select.

Google OAuth scopes — why they are needed

  • gmail.modify — required to archive, star, trash, and mark messages as read/unread. These are core email management actions initiated by you.
  • gmail.compose — required to create drafts and send replies from within the app.
  • userinfo.email — required to display your email address in the account switcher.

Data sharing

Mailmind does not sell, rent, or share your personal data or email content with any third party, except:

  • Your chosen email provider (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) — to retrieve and send mail, as directed by you
  • Your chosen AI provider — only when you explicitly trigger an AI feature, and only with the thread you have open at that moment

Data deletion and account removal

You can remove any connected account at any time in Settings → Manage accounts. This immediately deletes all stored tokens and credentials for that account from the local database. Revoking Mailmind's access from your Google Account security page (myaccount.google.com/permissions) also invalidates any stored tokens.

Uninstalling Mailmind removes the application. To also delete the local database, delete the data.db file from the installation folder.

Children's privacy

Mailmind is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy is updated, the new version will be distributed with the next app release and the "Last updated" date above will change. Continued use of the app after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Anonymous usage analytics

The Mailmind desktop app (v1.1.11 and later) counts how many people are using the app. This feature is on by default and can be turned off at any time in Settings → Data & Privacy.

The only data sent is:

  • A random install ID (generated locally — not linked to your name, email, or any personal information)
  • App version

No email address. No mail content. No personal information of any kind.

To opt out, go to Settings → Data & Privacy and turn off "Share anonymous usage data".
